Safety Tips
Personal Safety:
  • Always stay alert and aware of your surroundings.
  • Do not share personal information with strangers.
  • Keep your doors locked when at home.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.
  • If you feel unsafe, trust your instincts and leave the area immediately.
  • Walk in well-lit areas and avoid shortcuts through alleys or isolated places.
  • Carry a phone with emergency contacts saved, and have a way to call for help if needed.
  • Let someone know your whereabouts if you're going to a new place or meeting someone for the first time.
  • Be cautious of your surroundings when using ATMs, especially at night or in quiet areas.
  • If approached by someone suspicious, keep a safe distance and move to a populated area.
Online Safety:
  • Use strong passwords for online accounts.
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or emails.
  • Enable two-factor authentication where possible.
  • Be cautious when sharing personal information on social media. Avoid oversharing your whereabouts or plans.
  • Monitor your financial accounts regularly for any unusual activity.
  • Be wary of "phishing" scams that may trick you into providing personal information.
  • Set privacy settings on social platforms to limit who can see your posts and personal details.
Crime Awareness
Understanding Crime Types:
  • Violent crimes (e.g., assault, robbery, murder, domestic violence)
  • Property crimes (e.g., burglary, vandalism, theft, car theft)
  • Cybercrime (e.g., identity theft, hacking, phishing, online fraud)
  • Drug-related offenses (e.g., trafficking, possession, distribution)
  • White-collar crime (e.g., fraud, embezzlement, insider trading)
  • Organized crime (e.g., gangs, human trafficking, illegal trade)
Recognizing Warning Signs:
  • Unusual behavior in your neighborhood or workplace (e.g., frequent visitors at odd hours).
  • Suspicious individuals or vehicles lingering in areas without clear reasons.
  • Changes in the security or habits of your community, such as an increase in local thefts or break-ins.
  • Increased cyber activity on your accounts that you do not recognize.
  • Strange or inappropriate messages from unknown sources online (e.g., unsolicited requests for personal information).
Preventative Measures:
  • Secure your home with quality locks, alarms, and lighting.
  • Be cautious of sharing personal information, both in person and online.
  • Invest in cybersecurity, such as using strong passwords and two-factor authentication.
  • When driving, keep your car doors locked, and be cautious of strangers asking for help or information.
  • Keep an eye out for your neighbors and community; consider joining or forming neighborhood watch programs.
What to Do if You Are a Victim of Crime:
  • Immediately report the crime to local law enforcement. Provide as much detail as possible.
  • Preserve evidence (e.g., take photos, keep receipts, retain messages) if applicable.
  • Contact your bank and credit card companies if your financial information has been compromised.
  • For online crime, report suspicious activity to the platform and consider filing a report with your local authorities.

Being aware of crime patterns and knowing how to respond can help reduce your risk and keep your community safe. Always stay vigilant and trust your instincts if something feels off.
